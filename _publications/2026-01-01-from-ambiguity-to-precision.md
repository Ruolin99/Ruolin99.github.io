The Digitized Traffic Rule Optimization Framework is a specialized framework for safety-compliant automated driving trajectory planning under mixed human-automated vehicle ambiguous traffic scenarios. The proposed method integrates Metric Temporal Logic (MTL) formalization with NSGA-II genetic algorithm parameter calibration to convert vague natural-language traffic regulations into machine-executable quantified rules while optimizing driving safety, efficiency and ride comfort.

The framework addresses critical challenges in automated vehicle compliance by resolving semantic ambiguity of traffic laws and improving cross-scenario generalizability. The digitization pipeline combines knowledge-based rule parsing for fuzzy traffic provisions with data-driven optimization that leverages real-world safety-critical trajectory data to calibrate robust safety thresholds.

**Key contributions:**
- Ambiguity-resolved digitization framework for intersection traffic rules integrating domain knowledge reasoning and data-driven optimization
- Formalized traffic rule representation via Metric Temporal Logic with quantifiable spatial-temporal safety parameters
- Autonomous trajectory planning embedded with calibrated traffic rules supporting comprehensive multi-dimensional performance evaluation
- Strong generalizable safety improvement across multiple public driving benchmarks and diverse real-world intersection scenarios
